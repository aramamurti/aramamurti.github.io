About Me

Hi! I'm a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Nuclear Theory Group of the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Stony Brook University, under the supervision of Prof. Edward Shuryak. I am currently working on numerical simulations of magnetic and electric quasiparticles in Quark Gluon Plasma, in an effort to describe and understand the phenomenon of confinement. You can see and download the path-integral Monte Carlo C++ code for these simulations on my GitHub page.

Description

In the context of Improved Holographic QCD (IHQCD), we study the scalar sector of hadronic spectroscopy, identifying “glueballs” and “scalar mesons,” and calculate the degree of their mixing. QCD strings, holographic images of fundamental strings have a “gluonic core” and a “sigma cloud,” the latter of which generates σ exchanges and collectivization of the strings, affecting the chiral condensate and the minimum of the effective string potential, responsible for the very existence of the QCD strings. We run dynamical simulations of the multistring systems in the “spaghetti” setting approximating central pA collisions, and specify conditions for their collectivization into a black hole, or the dual quark gluon plasma fireball. Physics AdS/QCD Strings

Description

In the context of Improved Holographic QCD (IHQCD), the three-graviton interaction is deduced from the Einstein-Hilbert action. Using this structure in the cross section of double-Pomeron production of the tensor glueball, we calculate certain angular distributions of production and compare them with those from the CERN WA102 experiment. We find that the agreement is very good for the f2(2300) tensor glueball candidate. At the same time, other tensor states -- such as f2(1270) and f′2(1520) -- have completely different distributions. Physics AdS/QCD Pomeron
